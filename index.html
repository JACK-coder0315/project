Reflection and Analysis

The first two visualizations (A1 & A2) support the claim by highlighting that most complaints are unsubstantiated, both city-wide and in the highest-volume precincts. A1 uses a red bar chart to emphasize the alarming proportion of dismissed complaints, while A2 leverages a diverging bar layout to contrast substantiated vs. unsubstantiated outcomes across multiple precincts.

In contrast, the second pair (B1 & B2) challenges the claim by showcasing geographical distribution. B1 uses a green-tinted precinct map with higher substantiation rates to imply meaningful follow-up in many areas. B2 adopts a dot-density map to visually reinforce that accountability actions are widespread.

Personally, I find the second set (B1 and B2) more persuasive. The geographic framing and green visual palette give a sense of systemic response and fairness.

Deceptive techniques used:
- Color symbolism: red = failure, green = success
- Emphasis shift: A1/A2 highlight proportion; B1/B2 highlight distribution and absolute counts
- Spatial framing: B1 hides context of total complaints and focuses only on confirmed ones
